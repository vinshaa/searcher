Primarily, WebCL allows web applications to actualize speed with multi-core CPUs and GPUs; this intends to make computationally intensive programs feasible in the browser, e.g. physics engines, canvas element and video editing. WebCL is designed and maintained by the non-profit Khronos Group.[2] The completed and ratified specification for WebCL 1.0 was released on March 19, 2014.[3]    Contents  1 Implementation 1.1 Currently reflect the WebCL working draft 2 Related Browser Bugs3 References4 See also5 External links   Implementation[edit] Currently, no browsers natively support WebCL, because it is new. However, non-native add-ons are used to implement WebCL.
