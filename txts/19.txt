The {{Citation needed}} template aims to promote accountable discourse.   To ensure that all Wikipedia content is verifiable, anyone may question an un-cited claim by inserting a simple {{Citation needed}} tag, or by using a more comprehensive {{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=August 2015}} clause.  Example: 65% of people believe in ghosts.[citation needed]  Exercise caution before relying upon unsourced claims.If you can provide a reliable source for the claim, please be bold and replace the "Citation needed" template with enough information to locate the source. You may leave the copyediting to someone else, or learn more about citing sources on Wikipedia. Wikipedia:Referencing for beginners provides a brief introduction on how to reference Wikipedia articles.If someone tagged your contributions with {{Citation needed}} and you disagree, discuss the matter on the article's discussion page.Controversial, poorly sourced claims in biographies of living people should be deleted immediately.
